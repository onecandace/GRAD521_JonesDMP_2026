# Data Description
My social science research in the College of Forestry focuses on human forest relationships and conservation behavior. For this study I will be analyzing the potential impact of a novel 10 weekend woodland program designed for intergenerational participation. 
# Roles and Responsibilities
This research is not funded by grants or private stakeholders. I am responsible for data collection, analysis, storage and overall management. My primary advisor will be supervising DMP implementation and have access to locked files for the purpose of reviewing methods, data analysis, and assessment measures.  The fellowship I am under does not require a contingency plan for unforeseen relocation. If either myself or my advisor leaves the institution, I will continue the work and look for funding sources as needed. If potential funders require a different management plan, we would revisit the current plan and negotiate any changes pertinent to moving the project forward. 
# Data Standards and Metadata
IRB compliance is of primary concern because I will be working with human subjects and the ethical responsibility of posing no harm to participants is the primary concern. Measures will be taken by the researcher to ensure anonymity, protect privacy, and safely secure data.
# Storage and Security
We estimate that the capacity of storage needed for this study is approximately 720MB interviews, 20GB for images, and 50MB for dissertation notes, written texts, and graphs. During the investigative process, data will be stored in locked files on my work computer and in password accessible cloud files shared by me and my lead advisor. Locked thumb drives will provide additional digital copies that are not bound to network functionality. For further backup, files that do not contain identifying participant information will be printed and stored securely by the lead researchers.
# Access and Data Sharing
Once the dissertation papers are published the data will be publicly available via OSU’s repository and the repositories managed by potential academic publishers.
# Archiving and Preservation
The network saved files are backed up by Microsoft and Google operating systems. Upon graduation the metadata will be publicly available via the universitiy's respository. In addition, I will continue to maintain printed records and thumb drives for research preservation.
